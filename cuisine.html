Kazakh Dishes

It's a rich blend of flavors, heavily influenced by the use of meat, particularly from livestock like horses, sheep, and cattle. Dairy products, grains, and hearty vegetables also play significant roles in Kazakh dishes.

Beshbarmak

Beshbarmak, which means "five fingers" in Kazakh, is a revered dish symbolizing hospitality and togetherness. It traditionally consists of boiled meat (usually lamb or horse meat) served on a bed of flat noodles, topped with onion sauce.

Beshbarmak Recipe

Ingredients:
500g wide flat noodles
1 kg lamb or beef
3-4 onions
Water
Instructions:
Boil the Meat: In a large pot, cook the meat with water, salt (around 2 hours). Prepare Noodles: Cook noodles separately in salted boiling water according to package instructions. Caramelize Onions: Slice onions thinly and cook in vegetable oil until golden brown and caramelized.

Plov

Plov is a fragrant rice dish cooked with meat (often lamb or beef), vegetables, and aromatic spices. It's a staple in Kazakh cuisine and signifies generosity and celebration, often served during special occasions.

Plov Recipe

Ingredients:
2 cups long-grain rice
500g cubed lamb or beef
2 onions, sliced
3 carrots, julienned
4 cups broth
Instructions:
Brown meat, set aside. Saut√© onions, add carrots. Toast spices, return meat, add garlic. Add rice, broth, salt. Simmer covered for 20-25 mins. Optionally add raisins, dried apricots, or chickpeas. Fluff before serving hot. Adjust spices to taste. Enjoy!

Kurt (Dried Yogurt)

Kurt is a traditional dried dairy product made from fermented milk. It's shaped into small balls and dried, resulting in a unique and tangy flavor. Kurt serves as a portable and nutritious snack, reflecting the nomadic heritage of the Kazakh people.

Kurt Recipe

Ingredients:
Yogurt (preferably full-fat)
Instructions:
Take yogurt and strain it through a cheesecloth or muslin cloth to remove excess moisture. Shape the strained yogurt into small balls or cylinders. Place these shapes in a dry, well-ventilated area or in a dehydrator. Allow them to dry completely. This process can take a few days to a week, depending on the climate and humidity. Once completely dried, kurt becomes a tangy, chewy snack. Store it in an airtight container.

Kymyz (Fermented Mare's Milk)

Revered as a symbol of health and vitality, Kymyz is believed to have various health benefits, rich in vitamins, enzymes, and probiotics. It's considered a cultural heritage and a part of Kazakh identity, often served to guests as a gesture of hospitality.

Shubat (Fermented Camel's Milk)

another fermented dairy product prevalent among Kazakh and Central Asian nomadic cultures. It's made by fermenting camel's milk and has been a dietary staple for centuries, especially among nomadic tribes.

Kozhe (Fermented Cereal Drink)

traditional Kazakh fermented beverage made from cereals like wheat, barley, or millet. It's prepared by fermenting boiled grains with yeast and sugar.
